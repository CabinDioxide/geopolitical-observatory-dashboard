引言Premise

伊朗战争的走向，不仅可能影响伊朗政权本身的维系情况和中东其它国家的稳定，还有可能通过影响霍尔木兹海峡航道进一步波及全球油气和半导体供应链，将风险传导到更多脆弱国家。本沙盘推演拟先模拟战争的最坏情况，并由此倒推出这条传导路径上的主要观察节点。

我们初步认为，目前以色列、美国和伊朗的和平谈判很可能不会持续并取得成果，战争可能延续，并可能在全球范围内引发连锁反应。这一判断基于对 两个主要变量（伊朗政权脆弱、以色列战略目标未达成）和 两个次要变量（MAGA 分裂、中俄态度）的理解。

The trajectory of the Iran war affects more than just the regime's survival and Middle East stability. By controlling the Strait of Hormuz, it can transmit risk to global oil, gas, and semiconductor supply chains — and from there to a long list of vulnerable countries. This sandbox first models the worst case, then works backward to identify the main observation nodes along the transmission path.

Our initial assessment: the current Israel–US–Iran peace negotiations are unlikely to hold or produce results. The war will probably continue and could trigger global chain reactions. This judgment rests on two primary variables (Iran regime fragility, Israel's strategic objectives unmet) and two secondary variables (MAGA fracture, China–Russia posture).

SECTION 1 · 主要变量 SECTION 1 · PRIMARY VARIABLES

决定方向的两个变量 The Two Variables That Set Direction

这两个变量直接决定战争是否延续、海峡命运如何。 These two variables directly determine whether the war continues and what happens to the strait.

1.1 伊朗政权仍十分脆弱 1.1 The Iranian Regime Remains Highly Fragile

最高领袖宗教地位不稳

1979 年伊斯兰革命后，鲁霍拉·霍梅尼（Ruhollah Khomeini）建立起一套神权政体。霍梅尼在伊朗的国家机器之外设计了一套神权控制体系——在总统、议会、定期选举和最高法院之上设立 "最高领袖"（Supreme Leader）一职，对国家进行监护，直接控制武装力量、司法、国家广播、革命卫队、对外宣战权力。

按照霍梅尼当时的设计，最高领袖本身应是神学权威——在什叶派中称 马尔贾（Marja' / Ayatollah al-Uzma，大阿亚图拉），全球仅 5-7 人具备此资格。但霍梅尼去世后，继任的哈梅内伊只是中级阿亚图拉。1989 年通过宪法修正案去掉了"最高领袖必须是马尔贾"的要求1，并通过阿亚图拉理事会（Assembly of Experts）"集体追认"哈梅内伊为马尔贾级，他才得以顺利接班。

哈梅内伊 2026-02-28 在以色列空袭中死后2，他的次子莫杰塔巴（Mojtaba Khamenei）接班。但莫杰塔巴宗教身份更低——只是 Hojatoleslam（中级教士）级别3。虽然他已被阿亚图拉理事会选为最高领袖，但什叶派现在世且地位最高的马尔贾是 伊拉克纳杰夫的西斯塔尼（Sistani），他与哈梅内伊关系一直微妙，目前没有从宗教上背书莫杰塔巴接班。莫杰塔巴接任后 3 个月未公开露面4，地位仍不稳固。

革命卫队（IRGC）

哈梅内伊当年因权威不足，扩充了 伊斯兰革命卫队（Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps，IRGC） 来巩固自己的地位。IRGC 可以被理解为服从最高领袖指示的禁军，包含 5 个军种5：

  • 陆军（IRGC Ground Forces）
  • 航空航天军（Aerospace Force）——同时管理空军和战略弹道导弹
  • 海军（IRGC Navy）——独立于正规海军，专门负责波斯湾不对称作战
  • 圣城旅（Quds Force）——海外特种行动部门，主管对外代理人战争（胡塞武装、真主党、哈马斯、伊拉克什叶派民兵）
  • Basij 民兵

IRGC 还控制伊朗约 30-50% 的工业（旗下旗舰公司 Khatam al-Anbiya 主导建筑、能源服务、电信、汽车等领域）6以及国家媒体的一部分。

哈梅内伊死后 IRGC 的反应将左右国家未来。当前实际权力结构：莫杰塔巴名义、新任 IRGC 总司令 Ahmad Vahidi（2026-03-01 紧急上任，前任 Pakpour 与哈梅内伊同日被杀7）主导决策。Vahidi 是 1988-1997 圣城旅首任司令8，圣城旅现任司令 Qaani 是他的"旧部"，所以 IRGC 总部和圣城旅当前是同源协作而非对抗关系。如果这套结构出现内部分裂，伊朗有很大概率陷入分裂并走向内战。

民族矛盾

伊朗的主体民族是波斯人，约占伊朗人口 61%。其他 39% 分布如下9：

  • 阿塞拜疆人 16%——主要在伊朗西北，跟北边阿塞拜疆共和国是同族，多数什叶派
  • 库尔德人 10%——伊朗西部，跟伊拉克、土耳其、叙利亚的库尔德人同族，多数逊尼派
  • 洛尔人（Lurs）6%——伊朗西部山区
  • 阿拉伯人 2%——主要在伊朗西南胡齐斯坦省，多数逊尼派
  • 俾路支人 2%——伊朗东南，跟巴基斯坦、阿富汗的俾路支人同族，多数逊尼派

这种民族结构让"伊朗瓦解"在理论上是可能的——如果中央权威崩溃，少数民族聚居区可能寻求独立或自治。

需要特别关注 胡齐斯坦（Khuzestan）——伊朗西南、紧邻伊拉克的省份。这里集中了伊朗 80% 的陆上石油储量（占伊朗总储量 57%）10。胡齐斯坦的人口主要是阿拉伯族裔，2019 年汽油涨价抗议和 2021 年水危机抗议11都是这一带的事件。如果伊朗中央权威崩溃，胡齐斯坦是最可能脱离控制的地区——而它一旦失控，伊朗的石油经济立刻崩溃。

The Supreme Leader's religious authority is unstable

After the 1979 Islamic Revolution, Ruhollah Khomeini built a theocratic system. Outside the regular state apparatus he designed a separate clerical control structure, placing a "Supreme Leader" position above the president, parliament, periodic elections, and the supreme court — a guardianship over the state, with direct control over the armed forces, judiciary, national broadcasting, the Revolutionary Guards, and the power to declare war.

Per Khomeini's original design, the Supreme Leader had to be a top theological authority — what Shia Islam calls a Marja' (Ayatollah al-Uzma, Grand Ayatollah), of which only 5–7 exist worldwide. But when Khomeini died, his successor Khamenei was only a mid-rank ayatollah. The 1989 constitutional amendment removed the requirement that the Supreme Leader be a marja'1, and the Assembly of Experts collectively elevated Khamenei to marja' rank so his succession could proceed.

Khamenei was killed in an Israeli airstrike on 2026-02-282. His second son Mojtaba Khamenei took over, but his religious standing is even lower — only Hojatoleslam (mid-rank cleric)3. Although the Assembly of Experts has named him Supreme Leader, the world's most senior living marja' is Sistani in Najaf, Iraq. His relationship with Khamenei has long been delicate, and he has not endorsed Mojtaba's succession on religious grounds. Mojtaba has not appeared publicly in the three months since taking over4; his position is still shaky.

The Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC)

Because Khamenei lacked authority back then, he expanded the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) to consolidate his position. The IRGC functions as a praetorian force directly under the Supreme Leader, with 5 service branches5:

  • Ground Forces
  • Aerospace Force — handles both air operations and strategic ballistic missiles
  • Navy — separate from the regular navy, focused on asymmetric warfare in the Persian Gulf
  • Quds Force — overseas special operations, manages proxy warfare (Houthis, Hezbollah, Hamas, Iraqi Shia militias)
  • Basij paramilitary

The IRGC also controls roughly 30–50% of Iran's industry (its flagship Khatam al-Anbiya dominates construction, energy services, telecoms, automotive)6, plus a chunk of national media.

How the IRGC reacts after Khamenei's death determines the country's future. The current de facto power structure: Mojtaba in name, with new IRGC commander Ahmad Vahidi (emergency appointee on 2026-03-01 after his predecessor Pakpour was killed alongside Khamenei7) running decisions. Vahidi was the founding Quds Force commander 1988–19978; current Quds Force commander Qaani is essentially his former subordinate. So IRGC headquarters and the Quds Force are currently aligned — same lineage, no rivalry. If this structure fractures internally, Iran has a high probability of fragmenting toward civil war.

Ethnic tensions

Persians are the dominant ethnic group at roughly 61%. The other 39% is distributed as follows9:

  • Azerbaijanis 16% — northwestern Iran, ethnically tied to the Azerbaijan Republic to the north, mostly Shia
  • Kurds 10% — western Iran, related to Kurds in Iraq, Turkey, Syria, mostly Sunni
  • Lurs 6% — western mountain regions
  • Arabs 2% — mainly in Khuzestan in the southwest, mostly Sunni
  • Baloch 2% — southeastern Iran, ethnically tied to Baloch in Pakistan and Afghanistan, mostly Sunni

This ethnic structure makes "Iran's disintegration" theoretically possible — if central authority collapses, minority regions could seek independence or autonomy.

Pay particular attention to Khuzestan — the southwest province bordering Iraq. It holds 80% of Iran's onshore oil reserves (57% of total)10. Population is primarily Arab. The 2019 fuel-price protests and 2021 water crisis protests11 were both in this area. If central authority collapses, Khuzestan is the most likely region to slip out of control — and the moment it does, Iran's oil economy collapses immediately.

1.2 以色列战略目标尚未达成 1.2 Israel's Strategic Objectives Remain Unmet

自 1948 年以来，先后爆发过五次中东战争。其中除第一次（1948）和第四次（1973 赎罪日）是以色列被动应战以外，其它三次（1956 苏伊士、1967 六日、1982 黎巴嫩）都是以色列主动出击，且每次都取得一定战果（多次击败埃及，瓦解约旦、叙利亚和黎巴嫩支持巴勒斯坦的力量；埃及和约旦在美国斡旋下事实上不再成为以色列的军事对手）。

如果把 2023 年哈马斯袭击以色列到现在的伊朗战争看作 第六次中东战争，那么站在以色列的立场上，这场战争的最终战略目标应该是：

  • 确保伊朗不能获得核武器
  • 确保伊朗丧失威胁以色列本土的能力（包括长程弹道导弹和战机等）
  • 确保伊朗丧失发起代理人战争的能力（摧毁"抵抗之弧"——胡塞武装、真主党、哈马斯、伊拉克什叶派民兵）

目前伊朗方面提出的和平谈判条件离以色列战略目标相去甚远。当然，参考前三次中东战争，以色列未必能毕其功于一役。但目前以色列有一个相当充足的动机：他们评估 特朗普总统将是未来一段时间对以色列最为亲善的美国总统，趁特朗普在任期间彻底将伊朗无害化的动机非常强烈。

但是，以色列必须考虑特朗普的政治利益，尤其是中期选举。背后原因很直接：以军高度依赖美国的武器供应，尤其是萨德和爱国者防空导弹，但美国目前武器库已经遭受大量消耗——PAC-3 / THAAD / Patriot 三种反导拦截弹的战前总库存被消耗 45-50%12，无法同时兼顾俄乌战场和中东，更无法维持在台海的威慑。在霍尔木兹海峡封锁影响全球通胀的背景下，特朗普可能担心中期选举选情而暂时冻结战争。

然而，中期选举（2026-11-03）结束后，我们认为以色列有相当强的动机再度发起攻击——例如对莫杰塔巴或革命卫队的领导人再度发起斩首行动。摆脱了选民压力的特朗普很可能再度支持以色列的行动。综合评估，以色列在 2026-11 至 2028-01 的 lame duck 窗口内实施某种形式单方面升级的概率为 50-65%。

Five Arab-Israeli wars have occurred since 1948. The first (1948) and fourth (1973 Yom Kippur) were defensive for Israel; the other three (1956 Suez, 1967 Six-Day, 1982 Lebanon) were Israeli offensives. Each yielded substantive gains — Egypt was defeated multiple times; Jordan, Syria, and Lebanese forces supporting the Palestinians were broken; Egypt and Jordan have effectively ceased being military adversaries since US-mediated agreements.

If we treat the chain from the 2023 Hamas attack through the current Iran war as the Sixth Arab-Israeli War, then from Israel's perspective the strategic objectives are:

  • Ensure Iran cannot acquire nuclear weapons
  • Ensure Iran loses the capability to threaten Israeli soil (including long-range ballistic missiles and combat aircraft)
  • Ensure Iran loses the ability to wage proxy warfare (destroy the "Axis of Resistance" — Houthis, Hezbollah, Hamas, Iraqi Shia militias)

The peace terms Iran has put on the table fall far short of Israel's strategic objectives. Per the precedent of the previous three offensive wars, Israel may not achieve everything in one campaign. But Israel has a strong motivating factor: it assesses that President Trump will be the most pro-Israel US president it will see for the foreseeable future, so the incentive to fully neutralize Iran while Trump is in office is intense.

Israel must also weigh Trump's domestic political interests, particularly the midterms. The reason is direct: the IDF depends heavily on US weapons supply, especially THAAD and Patriot air-defense missiles. But US stockpiles have already been depleted — PAC-3 / THAAD / Patriot stockpiles are down 45–50% from pre-war levels12. The US cannot simultaneously cover Ukraine and the Middle East, much less maintain deterrence in the Taiwan Strait. With Hormuz disruption already feeding global inflation, Trump may freeze the war temporarily out of midterm electoral concerns.

But once the midterms (2026-11-03) are over, Israel has strong motivation to strike again — for example, fresh decapitation strikes on Mojtaba or IRGC senior leaders. A Trump freed from voter pressure is likely to back Israel again. Overall assessment: the probability of Israel implementing some form of unilateral escalation during the 2026-11 to 2028-01 lame duck window is 50–65%.

SECTION 2 · 次要变量 SECTION 2 · SECONDARY VARIABLES

影响节奏的两个变量 The Two Variables That Affect Pace

这两个变量虽然也对伊朗战争走向有较大影响，但不如主要变量决定性。 These also influence the trajectory but are not as decisive as the primary variables.

2.1 MAGA 的分裂 2.1 The MAGA Fracture

MAGA 是当下共和党的主体，他们团结在特朗普周围，但内部对伊朗战争存在四种立场。如果这四种立场裂痕进一步扩大，可能影响特朗普对以色列的支持态度：

MAGA is the current Republican Party's main body, united around Trump but split four ways on the Iran war. If these fractures widen, they could shift Trump's support for Israel:

孤立主义Paleo-Isolationists

反对美国介入任何海外战争。代表：班农、塔克·卡尔森、托马斯·马西。特朗普发动战争后这一派非常失望——卡尔森公开批评特朗普和鲁比奥，马西提案将美国以色列公共事务委员会（AIPAC）列为外国代理人。 Oppose all foreign military intervention. Figures: Bannon, Tucker Carlson, Thomas Massie. After Trump went to war this faction was deeply disappointed — Carlson publicly criticized Trump and Rubio; Massie introduced legislation to register AIPAC as a foreign agent.

务实强硬派Pragmatic Hawks

支持美国从现实利益出发打击伊朗政权。代表：海格塞斯、鲁比奥、斯蒂芬·米勒。这一派支持特朗普第一任期退出伊朗核协议并斩首苏莱曼尼。 Back US strikes on the Iranian regime from a realist perspective. Figures: Hegseth, Rubio, Stephen Miller. This faction supported Trump's first-term JCPOA withdrawal and the Soleimani assassination.

福音派锡安主义Evangelical Zionists

从神学上支持以色列。代表：迈克·约翰逊、林赛·格雷厄姆。基督教锡安主义把以色列视为圣经预言的核心，神学义务高于政策考虑。 Support Israel on theological grounds. Figures: Mike Johnson, Lindsey Graham. Christian Zionism treats Israel as central to biblical prophecy; theological obligation overrides policy calculation.

中立派Centrists / Post-Liberals

在伊朗问题上有保留，整体希望美国将更多资源用于本土。这一派主要围绕在 J.D. 万斯周围。万斯 2024 大选时反对新战争，现在作为副总统被迫公开支持战争。 Have reservations on Iran, generally want US resources directed toward domestic priorities. Centered around J.D. Vance. Vance opposed new wars during the 2024 campaign; as VP he is now forced to publicly support the war.

目前 MAGA 内部分裂程度的主要观测点是 万斯的态度——孤立主义和中立派都把万斯看作最重要的代表者。但我们判断这对伊朗战争和海峡命运的影响较小，原因：如果 MAGA 保持团结，特朗普继续坚定支持以色列概率很大；如果 MAGA 分裂，美国反而可能失去约束以色列的能力。因此不论出现何种情形，以色列都有较大可能继续采取强硬举措。

The main current observation point for the MAGA fracture is Vance's posture — both isolationists and centrists treat him as their key representative. But we judge this has limited impact on the Iran war and the strait's fate, because: if MAGA stays united, Trump's continued firm backing of Israel is likely; if MAGA fractures, the US instead loses its capacity to restrain Israel. Under either outcome, Israel continues to have strong likelihood of taking hardline action.

2.2 中俄的态度 2.2 China and Russia's Posture

在霍尔木兹海峡上，中国和俄罗斯都有支持伊朗政权的理由——如果伊朗能持续消耗美国军力，可以减轻两国的地缘政治承压。但两国动机的强烈程度不同。

中国：受限于海峡通畅性的双重诉求

中国与伊朗的经济合作动机强烈——2021 年签署的 25 年综合合作协议中，中方承诺投资约 4000 亿美元换伊朗折扣价石油；当前中国 13% 的石油进口来自伊朗。但 中国从根本上希望的是霍尔木兹海峡畅通——中国约 40% 的原油进口经此海峡13。

问题在于：中国对伊朗的持续军事支持反倒不一定能保证海峡畅通，反倒可能升级冲突并引发海峡长期封闭。因为伊朗在报复行动中也袭击了沙特、卡塔尔、阿联酋的炼化中心和军事基地（如 2026-03-18 Ras Laffan 事件）。这三国中，沙特有油气管道连接到红海（东西管线 5 mb/d 设计容量14），但卡塔尔和阿联酋的能源出口几乎完全依赖霍尔木兹。如果伊朗军事实力增强并与卡塔尔和阿联酋发生冲突，那也有可能导致霍尔木兹海峡封闭。

因此 中国可能通过更多的经济支持来避免伊朗现政权垮台，但没有强烈动机投入太多军事支持。

俄罗斯：受限于乌克兰战场的有心无力

俄罗斯与伊朗之间长期存在军事和情报合作。2022 年俄乌战争以来俄方依赖伊朗 Shahed-136 自杀式无人机（累计交付超 8000 架），2025-01 双方在莫斯科签《全面战略伙伴关系条约》。

但俄罗斯对伊朗的支持力度取决于乌克兰战场的进度。目前俄乌战争陷入胶着，而俄罗斯经济虽然没有恶化到危及政权存续的地步，但已经退化为传统的军工制造业，无力进一步投资先进科技产业。在这种情况下，俄罗斯就算想支持伊朗，很可能也在很大程度上有心无力。

On the Strait of Hormuz, both China and Russia have reasons to support the Iranian regime — Iran continuing to absorb US military attention reduces geopolitical pressure on both. But the strength of their motivations differs.

China: constrained by its dual need for the strait to flow

China's economic motivation is strong — the 25-year comprehensive cooperation agreement signed in 2021 commits ~$400B in Chinese investment in exchange for discounted Iranian oil; currently 13% of China's oil imports come from Iran. But what China fundamentally needs is for the Strait of Hormuz to stay open — about 40% of China's crude imports transit through it13.

The problem: sustained Chinese military support for Iran doesn't necessarily guarantee an open strait — it could actually escalate the conflict and cause prolonged closure. Because Iranian retaliation has hit refining centers and military bases in Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and the UAE (e.g., the 2026-03-18 Ras Laffan incident). Of these three states, Saudi Arabia has pipelines to the Red Sea (East-West pipeline at 5 mb/d design capacity14), but Qatar and the UAE rely almost entirely on the strait for energy exports. If Iran's military strength grows and conflict with Qatar/UAE worsens, it could close the strait.

Therefore China may use more economic support to prevent the current Iranian regime from collapsing, but has no strong incentive to commit significant military support.

Russia: constrained by Ukraine — willing but unable

Russia and Iran have long-standing military and intelligence cooperation. Since the 2022 Ukraine war, Russia has depended on Iranian Shahed-136 suicide drones (over 8,000 delivered cumulatively); the two signed a Comprehensive Strategic Partnership Treaty in Moscow in 2025-01.

But the level of Russian support for Iran depends on the Ukraine front. Currently the Russia-Ukraine war is in stalemate, and while the Russian economy hasn't deteriorated to the point of threatening regime survival, it has regressed into traditional military-industrial manufacturing, unable to invest further in advanced tech industries. So even if Russia wants to support Iran, it is largely unable to.

SECTION 3 · 传导链条 SECTION 3 · TRANSMISSION CHAIN

霍尔木兹海峡与全球供应链 The Strait of Hormuz and Global Supply Chains

伊朗战争主要影响伊朗政权存续和中东地缘，但霍尔木兹海峡的关闭却对全球供应链有严重影响。可能存在"政权维系但海峡关闭"（伊朗与卡塔尔/阿联酋冲突）或"政权垮台但海峡开放"（美军有效护航）的情形。 The Iran war mainly affects regime survival and Middle East geopolitics, but Hormuz closure has serious global supply-chain impact. Possible states include "regime survives but strait closed" (Iran vs Qatar/UAE conflict) and "regime falls but strait open" (effective US Navy escort).

3.1 油气供应链 3.1 Oil & Gas Supply Chain

全球约 30% 的海运石油和 20% 的液化天然气（LNG）经霍尔木兹海峡运输15。中国是世界上对霍尔木兹依赖最深的国家——约 40% 的原油进口经此海峡。要绕开霍尔木兹，唯一办法是在陆地上铺设输油管道，把海湾国家的石油直接送到红海或阿曼湾出口。

目前真正存在的"绕开管道"只有两条：

  • 沙特东西海岸管道：从波斯湾的 Abqaiq 油田到红海的 Yanbu 港，5 mb/d 设计容量（但满载工作时间有限）
  • 阿联酋 Habshan-Fujairah 管道（ADCOP）：从 Habshan 油田到阿曼湾 Fujairah 港，1.5-1.8 mb/d 设计容量

两条管道合计设计容量约 6.8 mb/d，但实际可立即调用的紧急绕行容量约 2.6 mb/d（管道平时不满载，需要逐步加压）。而霍尔木兹海峡战前每天通过的石油是 20.9 mb/d（2025 上半年 EIA 数据）。绕行容量是战前海峡流量的约 1/8。

当前实际状况（2026-05）：自 2026-02-28 美以联合空袭后伊朗事实封锁海峡。2026-04 月均通过量约为战前的 5%（vessel transponder 实测）20。2026-05-06 起开放通行近乎为零。海峡周边滞留商船 1,550+ 艘（战前日均通行约 138 艘）。也就是说海峡实质已经关闭三周。市场没有立刻崩盘，是因为沙特和阿联酋绕行管线满负荷运行 + 部分国家释放 SPR + 中印提前补库存——但这些缓冲只能撑 30-60 天。

比石油更严重的是 LNG。LNG 必须用专门的 LNG 船运输，不能用普通管道远距离跨海洋运输。卡塔尔是全球最大的 LNG 出口国之一（与澳大利亚、美国并列三大），而 卡塔尔的 LNG 出口 100% 经过霍尔木兹海峡，没有任何陆路替代。

About 30% of global seaborne oil and 20% of LNG transit the Strait of Hormuz15. China is the world's most Hormuz-dependent country — about 40% of its crude imports pass through. The only way to bypass Hormuz is via land pipelines that deliver Gulf oil directly to the Red Sea or the Gulf of Oman.

Only two genuine bypass pipelines exist:

  • Saudi East-West pipeline: from Abqaiq in the Persian Gulf to Yanbu on the Red Sea, 5 mb/d design capacity (limited hours at full load)
  • UAE Habshan–Fujairah (ADCOP): from Habshan oilfield to Fujairah on the Gulf of Oman, 1.5–1.8 mb/d design capacity

Combined design capacity is about 6.8 mb/d, but immediately deployable emergency bypass capacity is only ~2.6 mb/d (pipelines normally run below full load; pressure has to be ramped up gradually). Pre-war flow through the strait was 20.9 mb/d (EIA, H1 2025). Bypass capacity is about 1/8 of pre-war strait flow.

Actual current situation (2026-05): Iran has effectively closed the strait since the joint US-Israeli airstrikes of 2026-02-28. April 2026 monthly average throughput was about 5% of pre-war (per vessel transponder data)20. Since 2026-05-06 open transits have been near zero. 1,550+ commercial vessels are stranded around the strait (pre-war daily traffic was about 138). The strait has been substantively closed for three weeks. Markets haven't immediately crashed because the Saudi and UAE bypass pipelines are running at full load + some countries are releasing SPR + China and India pre-stocked — but these buffers only last 30–60 days.

More serious than oil is LNG. LNG requires specialized LNG carriers — it cannot be moved long-distance by pipeline across oceans. Qatar is one of the world's top three LNG exporters (alongside Australia and the US), and 100% of Qatar's LNG exports go through Hormuz; there is no overland alternative.

3.2 半导体供应链 3.2 Semiconductor Supply Chain

伊朗战争除了打击全球油气供应链外，还会通过三条路径影响全球半导体供应链：

以色列的半导体生产和设计

南部的 Kiryat Gat 是英特尔（Intel）的 18A fab，北部的 Yokneam 是英伟达（NVIDIA）的研发中心（负责数据中心 GPU 的网络芯片设计）16。北部的 Migdal HaEmek 有 Tower Semiconductor 的晶圆厂、Camtek 测试设备厂、Nova Measuring 量测设备厂——半导体行业的"配套生态"。

这些半导体工厂都在伊朗弹道导弹的有效射程内。此外，fab 这个东西对环境极度敏感——洁净室对震动、电压、温度都有严格要求。附近爆炸（即使没直接命中工厂）可能让光刻机失准、让在产芯片报废、让生产停滞数周。

台积电的液化天然气

半导体生产对电力的稳定性要求极高——电压闪烁千分之一秒就可能让在产芯片整批报废。台积电关键厂区使用专用的稳定电源，而台湾本身不产天然气，超过 96% 的能源依赖进口，LNG 占发电量约一半，其中 33.7% 来自卡塔尔17。台湾全岛 LNG 储备只够 11 天——一旦霍尔木兹海峡封闭，台湾的发电稳定性立刻面临风险。

半导体生产的特种气体

半导体生产除了需要硅片和电力，还需要多种"特种气体"——氖（neon）、氪（krypton）、氙（xenon）、三氟化氮（NF3）、氟（F2）等，用于光刻、刻蚀、清洗等关键工艺。每生产一片晶圆都需要消耗几种特种气体。

此前氖气全球约 70% 来自乌克兰 + 俄罗斯（这俩都是钢铁工业大国，氖是钢铁冶炼的副产品），但 2022 年俄乌战争后乌克兰氖气产能基本停滞。除俄乌以外，海湾国家和中国也是特种气体供应商。如果伊朗战争导致霍尔木兹海峡持续封锁，中国占特种气体的全球生产份额将从战前 25% 上升到 50% 以上。TSMC、三星、SK 海力士三家都在做"中国管制风险"压力测试，SK 海力士 2027 年产能预测已经下调 12%。

Beyond hitting global oil and gas, the Iran war affects global semiconductor supply chains through three paths:

Israel's semiconductor production and design

In the south, Kiryat Gat hosts Intel's 18A fab; in the north, Yokneam is NVIDIA's R&D center (designing networking chips for data-center GPUs)16. Also in the north, Migdal HaEmek hosts Tower Semiconductor's fabs, Camtek test equipment, and Nova Measuring metrology — the supporting ecosystem.

All these facilities are within range of Iranian ballistic missiles. Fabs are extremely environment-sensitive — cleanrooms require tight tolerances on vibration, voltage, temperature. A nearby explosion (even without a direct hit) can throw lithography out of alignment, scrap in-process wafers, and stop production for weeks.

TSMC's liquefied natural gas

Semiconductor production demands extreme power stability — a 1-millisecond voltage flicker can scrap an entire wafer batch. TSMC's critical fabs use dedicated stable power, and Taiwan produces no natural gas — over 96% of energy is imported, with LNG providing about half of electricity generation, of which 33.7% comes from Qatar17. Taiwan-wide LNG stockpiles cover only 11 days — Hormuz closure puts Taiwan's power stability at immediate risk.

Specialty gases for semiconductor production

Semiconductor production needs not only silicon wafers and power but also specialty gases — neon, krypton, xenon, NF3, F2, etc. — for lithography, etching, cleaning. Each wafer consumes multiple specialty gases.

Pre-2022, about 70% of global neon came from Ukraine + Russia (both steel-industry giants; neon is a byproduct of steel smelting). The 2022 Ukraine war essentially halted Ukrainian neon production. Apart from Russia/Ukraine, Gulf states and China are also specialty-gas suppliers. If the Iran war causes sustained Hormuz closure, China's share of global specialty-gas production rises from pre-war 25% to over 50%. TSMC, Samsung, and SK Hynix are all running "China regulatory risk" stress tests; SK Hynix has already cut its 2027 production forecast by 12%.

3.3 其它关键链条 3.3 Other Critical Chains

食品安全

化肥 90% 用天然气作原料。霍尔木兹海峡关闭 → LNG 价格暴涨 → 化肥价格暴涨 → 全球粮食价格暴涨，传导链清晰且无可阻断。

海运保险

所有商船都需要保险。战争风险险（war risk insurance）平时几乎免费（保费占货值 0.02%），战时跳升到 0.5-1.5%。伦敦劳合社（Lloyd's of London，1688 年成立，全球航运保险的核心市场）已经把整个波斯湾列为"高风险水域"。私人保险市场可能撤退，迫使美国政府的国际开发金融公司（DFC）做"最后保险人"——已经联合 Chubb 等承保商投入 400 亿美元再保险设施18。但截至 2026-05 这个设施尚未承保过一艘船——因为只覆盖美军护航船只，而护航编队规模有限。

Food security

90% of fertilizer uses natural gas as feedstock. Hormuz closure → LNG prices spike → fertilizer prices spike → global food prices spike. The transmission chain is clear and unblockable.

Maritime insurance

All commercial ships need insurance. War risk insurance is normally near-zero (0.02% of cargo value); in wartime it jumps to 0.5–1.5%. Lloyd's of London (founded 1688, the world's core marine insurance market) has already designated the entire Persian Gulf as a "high-risk area". Private insurance markets may withdraw, forcing the US Development Finance Corporation (DFC) to act as "insurer of last resort" — it has committed $40B in reinsurance facility jointly with Chubb and other underwriters18. But as of 2026-05 this facility has not insured a single vessel — because it only covers ships under US Navy escort, and the convoy program is limited in scale.

SECTION 5 · 最坏情景 SECTION 5 · WORST CASES

两条最坏的可能性 Two Worst-Case Paths

概率不高但破坏量级最大，是沙盘推演的核心警示。 Low probability, maximum damage — the core warnings of this sandbox.

最坏可能 1：伊朗政权崩溃 + 霍尔木兹海峡彻底关闭 Worst Case 1: Iranian Regime Collapse + Total Hormuz Closure

如果以色列升级攻击，伊朗现政权崩溃且陷入内战，内战进一步波及到周边的沙特、卡塔尔、阿联酋、科威特、伊拉克、埃及、阿塞拜疆、土耳其和阿富汗等地区，导致战乱进一步扩大，且霍尔木兹海峡遭到彻底关闭。地缘政治危机和供应链危机沿两条传导链条同时爆发。

更乱的中东

革命卫队分裂后，大量部队失去控制，与恐怖主义和极端宗教派别相结合，革命卫队武器流出到胡塞武装、巴勒斯坦解放组织、圣战军乃至其它机构，对以色列、伊拉克、叙利亚、黎巴嫩、沙特、卡塔尔、阿联酋等周边国家发动袭击。此外，革命卫队很可能跟伊朗各少数民族武装部队开战，引发的冲突可能波及阿塞拜疆、阿富汗、伊拉克和土耳其。

因通胀危机陷入困境的国家

巴基斯坦

巴基斯坦 GDP 的 8% 来自海湾国家汇款，如果海湾国家陷入战争，巴基斯坦将损失大笔收入。此外，如果油价从 $70 跃至 $180-220，这将让 2024 年 IMF 与巴基斯坦签订的 EFF 项目无法持续。巴基斯坦会在 3 个月内耗尽外汇储备用于进口能源。这可能引发大规模街头抗议，导致军事政变，甚至造成国家分裂或与印度的全面战争。

埃及

埃及有四大经济支柱：运河、旅游、侨汇、石油。第六次中东战争已经导致胡塞武装对红海的袭击，埃及苏伊士运河的收入已经下降到战前的一半。旅游业自 2024 年以来已因加沙战争下降 40%，地区全面冲突会让旅游业再降 60%。埃及 GDP 约有 7-8% 来自海湾国家汇款（主要是沙特和阿联酋）19。最后，埃及 2022 年成为天然气净进口国后对霍尔木兹 LNG 严重依赖，油价 + LNG 价格会让燃料补贴体系（占财政支出 8%）无法承担。埃及可能再度爆发动荡，穆斯林兄弟会可能回归。

土耳其

土耳其面临四大问题：(1) 能源进口依赖俄国、阿塞拜疆、伊拉克和伊朗；(2) 里拉已经在恶性贬值，通胀率达到 60% 以上；(3) 制造业出口依赖海湾国家 + 北非 + 欧洲；(4) 库尔德区域可能因为伊朗政权瓦解而动荡加剧。

南亚 + 东南亚

斯里兰卡（2022 年）、孟加拉国（2024 年）和印尼（2025 年）刚遭遇国内政治动荡，缅甸则持续内战中，通胀会进一步恶化这些国家的处境。印度本身有大量贫困人口，可能因为通胀和化肥危机遭遇困难。菲律宾高度依赖海湾国家的侨汇，且石油高度依赖中东进口，是东南亚国家中因霍尔木兹海峡关闭遭遇最大危机的一个。泰国自身处在威权化过程中，预计将遭遇严重衰退和政治动荡。马来西亚自身出产石油，承压较小。

非洲国家

非洲对霍尔木兹海峡依赖程度最高的国家是 埃塞俄比亚、肯尼亚、苏丹、马达加斯加、毛里求斯、塞舌尔和南非，其中南非的韧性最强。北非和西非主要依赖大西洋盆地油气，不是 Hormuz 直接受害者。但非洲本身已经因为恐怖主义、分裂运动和俄乌战争波及爆发了多轮政权更迭，全球通胀和欧洲经济危机将恶化这些国家的处境。

欧洲国家

欧洲本身能源高度依赖俄国和中东进口。一旦霍尔木兹海峡遭到长期封锁，欧洲将迎来更漫长的经济寒冬，这有可能导致欧洲国家的政权持续极端化，欧洲经济和政治会呈现拉美化特征。

日本

日本是发达国家，但能源高度依赖中东。一旦霍尔木兹海峡出现危机，日本不会面临政治崩溃，但可能面临二战以来最大的政治转向：日本将全面右转并在五年内实现快速武装化。

必须强调，出现这种最坏情形的可能性非常小，因为前提是以色列全面升级战争，且美军决定不再护航霍尔木兹海峡。但这是海湾三国（沙特、卡塔尔、阿联酋）最恐惧的情形——沙特已经要求美国在升级任何战争举措前必须知会沙特，而卡塔尔和阿联酋则强烈要求，如果以色列升级战事，美军必须增强对海峡的控制和护航。因此，以色列升级战事和美军放弃对海峡护航，两者很难同时存在。

If Israel escalates, the current Iranian regime collapses and falls into civil war, with the war spilling into Saudi Arabia, Qatar, UAE, Kuwait, Iraq, Egypt, Azerbaijan, Turkey, Afghanistan, and Hormuz is fully closed. Geopolitical crisis and supply-chain crisis erupt along two transmission paths simultaneously.

A messier Middle East

After IRGC fractures, large units lose control and merge with terror groups and extremist religious factions. IRGC weapons flow to Houthis, the Palestine Liberation Organization, jihadist groups, and others, attacking Israel, Iraq, Syria, Lebanon, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, UAE, and other neighbors. The IRGC likely fights Iran's minority armed forces, with conflicts spilling into Azerbaijan, Afghanistan, Iraq, and Turkey.

Countries hit by inflation crisis

Pakistan

Pakistan's GDP draws 8% from Gulf remittances; if Gulf states fall into war, Pakistan loses major revenue. If oil jumps from $70 to $180–220, the 2024 IMF EFF program with Pakistan becomes unsustainable. Pakistan exhausts its forex reserves on energy imports within 3 months. This may trigger mass street protests, military coup, even national fragmentation or full-scale war with India.

Egypt

Egypt has four economic pillars: the Suez Canal, tourism, remittances, oil. The Sixth Arab-Israeli War's Houthi Red Sea attacks have already cut Suez Canal revenue to half of pre-war. Tourism has fallen 40% since 2024 due to Gaza; full regional conflict drops it another 60%. Egyptian GDP draws 7–8% from Gulf remittances (mainly Saudi and UAE)19. Finally, Egypt became a net gas importer in 2022, making it heavily dependent on Hormuz LNG; the combined oil + LNG price surge breaks the fuel subsidy system (8% of fiscal spending). Egypt may experience renewed unrest; the Muslim Brotherhood may return.

Turkey

Turkey faces four problems: (1) energy imports depend on Russia, Azerbaijan, Iraq, Iran; (2) the lira is already in malignant depreciation with 60%+ inflation; (3) manufacturing exports depend on Gulf states + North Africa + Europe; (4) Kurdish regions may face intensified unrest if the Iranian regime collapses.

South Asia + Southeast Asia

Sri Lanka (2022), Bangladesh (2024), and Indonesia (2025) recently experienced domestic political upheaval; Myanmar is in ongoing civil war; inflation worsens all of these. India itself has a large poor population that may struggle under inflation and fertilizer crisis. The Philippines is most exposed in Southeast Asia — high dependence on Gulf remittances and on Middle Eastern oil. Thailand is in the middle of authoritarianization and is expected to face severe recession and political upheaval. Malaysia produces its own oil so faces less pressure.

African countries

The African countries most exposed to Hormuz are Ethiopia, Kenya, Sudan, Madagascar, Mauritius, Seychelles, and South Africa, of which South Africa has the most resilience. North and West African states depend mainly on Atlantic basin oil and gas, so they are not direct victims. But Africa has already had multiple government changes from terrorism, separatist movements, and Ukraine-war spillovers; global inflation and a European economic crisis worsen these states' positions.

European countries

Europe is heavily dependent on Russian and Middle Eastern energy imports. Prolonged Hormuz closure brings a longer economic winter, potentially driving continued political radicalization. European economy and politics may take on "Latin Americanization" characteristics.

Japan

Japan is developed but heavily dependent on Middle Eastern energy. A Hormuz crisis does not bring political collapse, but may bring the largest political shift since WWII: Japan turns fully rightward and achieves rapid rearmament within five years.

It must be emphasized that this worst case has very low probability, because it requires both Israeli full-scale escalation and US Navy abandoning Hormuz escort. But this is the scenario that the Gulf trio (Saudi, Qatar, UAE) most fear — Saudi Arabia has already demanded prior US notification of any escalation, while Qatar and UAE strongly demand stronger US control and escort if Israel escalates. So Israeli escalation and US Navy abandonment of strait escort are unlikely to occur simultaneously.

最坏可能 2：美军精力被牵制 + 台海战争爆发 Worst Case 2: US Forces Pinned + Taiwan Strait War

美国对以色列的弹药支援消耗了 PrSM（精确打击导弹）库存的 45%、THAAD 拦截弹的 50%、"爱国者"拦截弹的 50%。这将导致美国对台湾、日本、韩国的 PAC-3 / THAAD 交付被推迟 18-36 个月。这可能导致中国在台海发动战争的可能性上升。一旦这种情形发生且不能马上结束并恢复秩序的话，全球半导体供应链将完全被中断，全球 GDP 损失在 10% 以上，科技进步的引擎将熄灭，日本必须介入，这将与中东战争叠加，导致二战以来最为毁灭性的局面爆发。

但是，发生这种情形的概率比第一种还要小。原因如下：

  • 美国军工产业的产能正在迅速扩张，国防生产法（Defense Production Act）正在令美国制造业进入准战时状态
  • 中国军队高层刚刚进行调整，指挥链重建需要时间
  • 中国经济正在承受房地产下行周期的压力，北京有大量内部问题要解决
  • 北京依然相信"东升西降"叙事，没有充分的理由进行地缘政治冒险
  • 日本再军事化的速度比预期要快，可能在北京重建指挥链之前建立起威慑能力

US munitions transfers to Israel have consumed 45% of PrSM (Precision Strike Missile) stockpile, 50% of THAAD interceptors, and 50% of Patriot interceptors. This delays PAC-3 / THAAD deliveries to Taiwan, Japan, and South Korea by 18–36 months — raising the probability that China launches a Taiwan Strait war. If that happens and isn't quickly contained, global semiconductor supply chains are fully severed, global GDP losses exceed 10%, the technology-progress engine stalls, Japan must intervene, and the situation overlaps with the Middle East war to produce the most destructive scenario since WWII.

But this scenario is even lower probability than the first. Reasons:

  • US defense industry capacity is rapidly expanding; the Defense Production Act is pushing US manufacturing into a quasi-wartime posture
  • China's military leadership has just been reorganized; rebuilding command chains takes time
  • China's economy is under property downturn pressure; Beijing has plenty of internal problems
  • Beijing still believes the "East rises, West falls" narrative — no sufficient reason for geopolitical adventures
  • Japanese rearmament is faster than expected, potentially establishing deterrence before Beijing finishes its command-chain rebuild
