方法论说明

本 dashboard 的核心任务是将地缘政治冲击（霍尔木兹海峡封锁）通过宏观与政治传导链转换为美国选举结果预测。下文逐项说明所用模型、选择理由、历史预测准确度、传导链结构与数据来源。

一、为什么选这三个模型？为什么没选其他模型？

美国选举预测领域约有 6-8 个有学术背书的模型。本 dashboard 挑选三个互补模型（Fair / Hibbs / Abramowitz）并构建集合预测，理由如下：

入选三个模型的具体公式

1. Fair Vote Equation（Ray Fair, Yale 1978），预测现任党两党票份额：

V_民主党 = 47.42 + 0.667·G·I + (-0.690)·P·I + 0.984·Z·I
        + 3.41·DPER + (-3.46)·DUR·I + (-0.105)·I

参数定义：
  G    = 选举年 Q1-Q3 实际人均国内生产总值（GDP）增长率（年化 %）
  P    = 任期内国内生产总值平减指数 15 季度增长率绝对值（年化 %）
  Z    = 任期内 15 季度中实际人均增长 >3.2% 的"好消息季度"数量（最大 15）
  DPER = 现任总统亲自参选系数（D=+1, R=-1, 开放席位=0）
  DUR  = 在位时长系数（首任=0, 二连任=1, 三连任=1.25...）
  I    = 党派虚拟变量（D=+1, R=-1）
均方根误差（RMSE）= ±2.5pp（全国两党票出样本）

2. Hibbs 面包与和平模型（Douglas Hibbs, 1982/2008），预测现任党两党票份额：

V_现任党 = 46.10 + 3.49·R + (-0.00355)·累计战争伤亡

参数定义：
  R = λ-加权 15 季度实际人均可支配收入（RDPI）增长率（年化 %）
  λ = 0.915（按季度几何衰减——最近季度权重最大）
  累计战争伤亡 = 任期内非主动战争中美军死亡人数（2026-2028 期间为 0）
均方根误差（RMSE）= ±1.85pp（全国两党票，1952-2012 历史样本）

3. Abramowitz Time-for-Change（Alan Abramowitz, 1988），预测现任党两党票份额：

V_现任党 = 51.40 + 0.108·G_Q2 + 0.642·NETAPP + (-4.0)·TERM2

参数定义：
  G_Q2    = 选举年第二季度实际国内生产总值（GDP）增长率（年化 %）
  NETAPP  = 现任总统 6 月净支持率（赞同率 - 反对率）
  TERM2   = 现任党连续执政是否 ≥2 任的虚拟变量（是=1, 否=0）
均方根误差（RMSE）= ±1.90pp

4. Bafumi-Erikson-Wlezien（BEW, 2010），预测中期众议院席位变化：

现任党席位变化 = -22 + 3.5·现任党通用国会选票领先 + 0.4·现任总统净支持率

参数定义：
  现任党通用国会选票领先 = 通用国会选票（Generic Ballot）现任党 - 在野党，pp 单位
  现任总统净支持率 = 赞同率 - 反对率，pp 单位
  -22 截距 = 历史中期选举现任党平均失席位
均方根误差（RMSE）= ±12 席

入选三个模型 + 一个中期模型的集体特征

模型核心变量历史 RMSE著名成功著名失败
Fair Vote Equation 综合：增长 + 通胀 + 结构 ±2.5pp 1980 卡特败、1984 里根赢、2008 麦凯恩败 1992：预测老布什赢 55%，实际败 46%（最著名翻车——经济数据 vs 选民感知背离）
Hibbs 面包与和平 极简：实际收入 + 战争伤亡 ±1.85pp 1952-2012 八个选举平均误差最低 2016（共和党选民关注"非经济"议题）
Abramowitz Time-for-Change 支持率主导：Q2 GDP + 6 月支持率 + 任期 ±1.90pp 2016 准确预测特朗普（Trump）赢；1988-2012 八届里六届方向对 2020 原版预测特朗普赢；作者本人加了主观修正

三个模型使用不同输入变量：Fair 综合考虑增长、通胀与结构因素；Hibbs 仅观察实际收入但加权 15 季度记忆；Abramowitz 主要看支持率（民意层）。三模型预测一致时可信度高；分歧大时（如当下 Fair 与 Abramowitz 差 11.7pp）则提示经济数据与选民感知出现严重背离，1992 和 2024 选举均属此种情形。

为什么没选其他常见模型？

未选模型排除理由
Lichtman 13 Keys to the White House 方法论：13 个二元判断（每个由分析师主观打分）。学界对其科学性争议大——2024 年预测哈里斯赢哈里斯（Harris）是其首次失败，揭穿"必胜"神话。排除原因：变量本质是定性主观判断，不可机械计算；不能纳入自动化 dashboard。
Norpoth Primary Model（Helmut Norpoth） 方法论：仅用初选表现 + 钟摆效应。声称 1912 年以来 25 次预测对 22 次。2016/2024 都正确预测特朗普赢。排除原因：变量选择被怀疑过拟合——25 次中错过几次的模式不可重复验证；初选数据 2027 年才有，对 2028 当下无前瞻价值。
Lewis-Beck-Tien Political Economy 方法论：Fair 模型的扩展，加入消费者信心 ICS 和"job approval × growth"交互项。排除原因：实际上是 Fair + ICS 的微调版本——增加 ICS 等于把"感知层"半重复纳入；与 Hibbs 和 Abramowitz 已经覆盖的感知通道高度相关，不增加独立信息。
Drew Linzer Dynamic Bayesian Model（2013） 方法论：贝叶斯州层模型 + Kalman filter 实时更新。Economist 2020 模型、Silver Bulletin 都用类似架构。排除原因：需要每周民调数据 + 州层模型，数据需求量大；本 dashboard 走的是"基本面 + 情景分析"路径，不是民调驱动。Linzer 类模型适合选举前 6 个月的预测窗口，不适合距大选 2.5 年的当下 2028。
Justin Grimmer 等人的"朴素预测"（2024） 方法论：用"上届结果 + 现任党"作基线，论证学术模型在统计上不优于此基线。排除原因：本身是元批评，不是预测工具；其结论已通过我们的"模型分歧 = 警报"机制吸收。
Allan Lichtman 之外的其他经济模型（如 Cuzán、Holbrook） 方法论：与 Fair / Hibbs / Abramowitz 高度相似的回归模型。排除原因：与已选三个模型的输入变量重叠度高，无法增加集合预测的方差降低收益（PollyVote 论文证明：相关性高的模型加入集合反而效果有限）。

选择原则总结：(1) 学术背书 + 公开公式可复现；(2) 输入变量分散（综合、收入、支持率），互补性最大化集合预测增益；(3) 当前数据可获取（不依赖未发布的初选数据）；(4) 出样本均方根误差（RMSE）≤2.5pp。

二、集合预测的方法与已知局限

原理：单一模型必有偏好；集合互补模型可降低平均误差。德国学者 Andreas Graefe 提出的 PollyVote 方法证明：将所有公开模型按"反向 RMSE"加权平均（历史误差小的模型权重大），出样本 RMSE 比任何单一模型低 30-40%。

本 dashboard 采用的具体计算：

权重 w_i = (1 / RMSE_i) / Σ(1 / RMSE_j)
集合预测 = Σ w_i × 模型_i 预测值
集合方差 = Σ w_i² × RMSE_i² （假设独立性）

当下权重：Hibbs ≈ 36%（RMSE 1.85），Abramowitz ≈ 36%（RMSE 1.90），Fair ≈ 27%（RMSE 2.50）。集合预测带宽约 ±1.1pp。

⚠️ 这种计算方法的已知问题：上述方差公式假设三个模型相互独立，但实际上 Fair / Hibbs / Abramowitz 都使用了重叠的宏观经济变量（GDP 增长、通胀、收入）。模型预测误差是正相关的，而非独立。

  • 真实集合 RMSE 高于公式计算值。真实值约在 ±1.5-2.0pp 区间（介于公式给出的 1.1pp 与最小单一模型 RMSE 1.85pp 之间）。Dashboard 显示的 ±1.1pp 是下限，不是真实值。
  • 模型分歧大时反而更可靠。当三个模型预测一致（说明输入信号强烈），集合预测可信度高。当三模型分歧大（如当下 Fair 与 Abramowitz 差 11pp），表明模型在"权重不同输入变量时给出不同结论"，这本身就是诊断信号——意味着经济数据与选民感知出现严重背离。
  • 修正建议：在解读集合预测时，应将名义 ±1.1pp 带宽放大约 50%，得到更现实的 ±1.5-2.0pp 不确定性。即"集合预测 R 48.4%"的真实置信区间约为 [46.4%, 50.4%]，而非 [47.3%, 49.5%]。
  • 未做修正的原因：精确计算模型预测误差的协方差矩阵需要每个模型在历史 60+ 选举上的逐年预测值，这些数据公开度有限，且 60 个样本估计 6 个协方差仍噪音很大。当前 dashboard 使用独立性假设的"乐观下限"，并通过这条 caveat 显式声明。

三、传导链：从油价到选票的九步链条

霍尔木兹冲击通过九步传导至选举结果，每一步对应一个可观测指标与传导弹性。所有弹性参数均从历史事件实测得出，非理论推导：

第几步变量来源到下一步的弹性
1布伦特原油FRED 日度$1/桶 → 汽油 +$0.025/加仑
2美国零售汽油FRED 周度$1/加仑 → 头条 CPI +0.85pp YoY
3战略石油储备EIA 周报缓冲容量 — 释放可压油价 ~$5-10
4头条 CPIFRED 月度（BLS）1pp → 密歇根 ICS -3.2 点
5核心 CPIFRED 月度追踪是否扩散到非能源
6实际可支配收入FRED 月度（BEA）Hibbs 模型核心输入
7密歇根消费者信心FRED 月度-10 点 → 总统支持率 -1.1pp
8总统支持率RCP 手动每周Abramowitz 核心输入；通用国会选票传导

链条总传导时滞约 4-9 个月。Dashboard 追踪每步压力分以识别传导阶段——例如当下油价（第 1 步）已进入告警，CPI（第 4 步）尚未充分传导；待 CPI 反映完毕时，整链将同步进入告警状态。

四、传导弹性的校准来源

采用四次历史石油冲击事件作为校准基准：

  • 1980 伊朗革命+两伊战争：油价 $14→$39（+179%），CPI 峰值 14.8%，ICS 75→51，卡特两党票 44.7% 惨败。当下霍尔木兹危机最直接的历史类比——同样波斯湾起源、同样持续时间长、最终消费者信心彻底崩溃。
  • 1990-91 海湾战争：油价 $17→$41（+141%），持续仅 5 个月。Bush 41 最终败给克林顿——证明短促油价冲击的政治后效可延续 18 个月。亦为 Fair 模型最著名失败案例。
  • 2008 油价峰值：油价 $60→$147（+145%），持续 8 个月。McCain 败 7.4pp。证明经济数据与选民感知双重恶化的破坏力。
  • 2022 俄乌战争：油价 $90→$128，叠加 Dobbs 判决抵消。证明累积通胀记忆可延续至下一选举周期（2024 哈里斯仍受拖累）。

六、Dashboard 的能力边界

  • 不输出 2028 大选州层预测。候选人未定（特朗普任期满，开放席位）、初选未开始、2.5 年外州层数据噪音远大于信号。任何具体 2028 州层数字均属假精度。
  • 不输出 2026 众议院选区层预测。选区级预测需 Cook PVI 选区数据 + 候选人质量 + 现任优势变量，需订阅 Cook Political Report。本 dashboard 仅提供州层估算。
  • 不输出"X% 概率赢 2028"单点预测。三模型集合 RMSE ±2.5pp，加上 2.5 年外的额外不确定性，单点估计具误导性。
  • 不预测非经济议题影响。如 Dobbs 判决（堕胎议题）等非经济变量不在任何模型内。该类变量在 2022 中期帮助民主党抵消油价冲击；2026 类似变量可能再现，但模型无法捕捉。
  • 不预测黑天鹅事件。新一轮 911 级恐袭、大型自然灾害、重大金融崩盘等均不在传导链内。

七、术语表

RMSE

1. Why these three models? Why not others?

The US election forecasting field has approximately 6-8 academically-backed models. This dashboard selects three complementary models (Fair / Hibbs / Abramowitz) and constructs an ensemble forecast for the following reasons:

Explicit formulas of the three selected models

1. Fair Vote Equation (Ray Fair, Yale 1978), predicts the in-party two-party vote share:

V_Democrat = 47.42 + 0.667·G·I + (-0.690)·P·I + 0.984·Z·I
        + 3.41·DPER + (-3.46)·DUR·I + (-0.105)·I

Variables:
  G    = real per-capita GDP growth, election-year Q1-Q3, annualized %
  P    = |GDP deflator growth| over administration's first 15 quarters, annualized %
  Z    = number of "good news quarters" (real per-capita growth >3.2%) in first 15 quarters (max 15)
  DPER = sitting president personally running (D=+1, R=-1, open seat=0)
  DUR  = duration penalty (first term=0, second consecutive=1, third=1.25...)
  I    = party dummy (D=+1, R=-1)
RMSE = ±2.5pp (national two-party vote, out-of-sample)

2. Hibbs Bread & Peace (Douglas Hibbs, 1982/2008), predicts the in-party two-party vote share:

V_incumbent = 46.10 + 3.49·R + (-0.00355)·cumulative war fatalities

Variables:
  R = λ-weighted real per-capita disposable income (RDPI) growth over 15 quarters, annualized %
  λ = 0.915 (geometric quarterly decay — most recent quarter weighted highest)
  Cumulative war fatalities = US military deaths in non-defensive wars during the term (0 for 2026-2028)
RMSE = ±1.85pp (national two-party vote, 1952-2012 historical sample)

3. Abramowitz Time-for-Change (Alan Abramowitz, 1988), predicts the in-party two-party vote share:

V_incumbent = 51.40 + 0.108·G_Q2 + 0.642·NETAPP + (-4.0)·TERM2

Variables:
  G_Q2    = real GDP growth in election-year Q2, annualized %
  NETAPP  = sitting president's June net approval (approve - disapprove)
  TERM2   = dummy for in-party serving 2+ consecutive terms (yes=1, no=0)
RMSE = ±1.90pp

4. Bafumi-Erikson-Wlezien (BEW, 2010), predicts midterm House seat change:

In-party seat change = -22 + 3.5·in-party Generic Ballot lead + 0.4·sitting president's net approval

Variables:
  In-party Generic Ballot lead = Generic Ballot for in-party - opposition, in pp
  Sitting president's net approval = approval - disapproval, in pp
  -22 intercept = historical average midterm in-party seat loss
RMSE = ±12 seats

Comparative characteristics of the three selected models

ModelCore variablesRMSEFamous successesFamous failures
Fair Vote Equation Comprehensive: growth + inflation + structure ±2.5pp 1980 Carter loss, 1984 Reagan win, 2008 McCain loss 1992: predicted Bush 41 win at 55%, actually lost at 46% (most famous failure — economic data vs voter perception divergence)
Hibbs Bread & Peace Minimalist: real income + war casualties ±1.85pp Lowest average error across 1952-2012, 8 elections 2016 (Republican voters focused on "non-economic" issues)
Abramowitz Time-for-Change Approval-led: Q2 GDP + June approval + term penalty ±1.90pp 2016 correctly predicted Trump win; 1988-2012 directionally correct in 6 of 8 elections 2020 original predicted Trump win; author manually adjusted

The three models use different input variables: Fair jointly considers growth, inflation, and structural factors; Hibbs tracks only real income but with 15-quarter weighted memory; Abramowitz primarily reflects approval (the public-opinion layer). When all three agree, confidence is high; when they diverge sharply (currently Fair vs Abramowitz differ by 11.7pp), this signals serious data-perception divergence — both 1992 and 2024 were such cases.

Why other common models were not selected

Excluded modelRationale
Lichtman 13 Keys to the White House Methodology: 13 binary judgments (each subjectively scored by analyst). Significant academic dispute about scientific validity — 2024 was its first failure (predicted Harris would win), debunking the "always correct" narrative. Excluded because: variables are inherently qualitative subjective judgments, not mechanically computable; cannot be incorporated into an automated dashboard.
Norpoth Primary Model (Helmut Norpoth) Methodology: uses primary performance + pendulum effect only. Claims 22 of 25 correct since 1912. Correctly predicted Trump in 2016 and 2024. Excluded because: variable selection and sample period are suspected of overfitting — the few errors among 25 are not robustly identifiable; primary data won't exist until 2027 and provides no forward-looking value for 2028 today.
Lewis-Beck-Tien Political Economy Methodology: extension of Fair, adds consumer sentiment (ICS) and "approval × growth" interaction. Excluded because: essentially Fair + ICS — adding ICS overlaps the perception channel that Hibbs and Abramowitz already capture, providing little independent information.
Drew Linzer Dynamic Bayesian Model (2013) Methodology: Bayesian state-level model + Kalman filter for live updates. The Economist 2020 model and Silver Bulletin use similar architecture. Excluded because: requires weekly polling data + state-level model; data demands are heavy. This dashboard uses a "fundamentals + scenario analysis" approach, not a polling-driven one. Linzer-style models are appropriate for the final 6-month window before an election, not 2.5 years out.
Justin Grimmer et al. "Naive Forecast" (2024) Methodology: uses "previous result + incumbent party" as baseline, argues academic models are statistically indistinguishable from this baseline. Excluded because: this is a meta-critique, not a forecasting tool; its conclusion is already absorbed via our "model disagreement = warning" mechanism.
Other economic models (Cuzán, Holbrook, etc.) Methodology: regression models highly similar to Fair / Hibbs / Abramowitz. Excluded because: input variables overlap heavily with selected models, providing no incremental variance reduction in the ensemble (PollyVote literature shows that highly-correlated models contribute little to ensemble gains).

Selection principles summarized: (1) Academic backing + publicly available formulas; (2) Diverse input variables (comprehensive, income, approval) to maximize ensemble complementarity; (3) Currently available data (no dependence on yet-to-be-released primary data); (4) Out-of-sample RMSE ≤ 2.5pp.

2. Ensemble forecast methodology and known limitations

Principle: Single models always have biases; combining complementary models reduces average error. The PollyVote method, proposed by German researcher Andreas Graefe, has shown that weighting all public models by inverse RMSE (smaller historical error → larger weight) produces an out-of-sample RMSE 30-40% lower than any single model.

Specific calculation used in this dashboard:

Weight w_i = (1 / RMSE_i) / Σ(1 / RMSE_j)
Ensemble forecast = Σ w_i × Model_i prediction
Ensemble variance = Σ w_i² × RMSE_i² (assuming independence)

Current weights: Hibbs ≈ 36% (RMSE 1.85), Abramowitz ≈ 36% (RMSE 1.90), Fair ≈ 27% (RMSE 2.50). Ensemble band approximately ±1.1pp.

⚠️ Known issue with this calculation: the variance formula above assumes the three models are independent, but in reality Fair / Hibbs / Abramowitz all use overlapping macroeconomic variables (GDP growth, inflation, income). Their prediction errors are positively correlated, not independent.

  • Real ensemble RMSE is higher than the formula value. The true value is approximately ±1.5-2.0pp (between the formula's 1.1pp and the smallest single-model RMSE of 1.85pp). The dashboard displays ±1.1pp as the lower bound, not the true value.
  • Disagreement among models is itself a more reliable signal. When all three agree (input signal is strong), ensemble confidence is high. When models diverge sharply (currently Fair vs Abramowitz differ by 11pp), this indicates "models give different answers because they weight inputs differently" — itself a diagnostic signal, indicating economic data and voter perception are sharply divergent.
  • Recommended adjustment: when interpreting the ensemble, scale the nominal ±1.1pp band by ~50% to get a more realistic ±1.5-2.0pp uncertainty. So "ensemble forecast R 48.4%" has a true confidence interval of approximately [46.4%, 50.4%], not [47.3%, 49.5%].
  • Reason for not formally correcting: precise computation of the model error covariance matrix requires year-by-year predictions across 60+ historical elections; public data is limited and 60 samples to estimate 6 covariances is still noisy. The dashboard uses the independence-assumption "optimistic lower bound" and discloses this caveat explicitly.

3. Transmission chain: nine steps from oil prices to votes

A Hormuz shock propagates through nine steps to electoral outcomes. Each step has a corresponding observable indicator and a transmission elasticity. All elasticity parameters are empirically derived from historical events, not theoretical derivations:

By the time CPI catches up, the entire chain will be in alarm simultaneously.

4. Calibration sources for transmission elasticities

Four historical oil-shock events serve as calibration anchors:

  • 1980 Iranian Revolution + Iran-Iraq War: oil $14→$39 (+179%), CPI peak 14.8%, ICS 75→51, Carter two-party share 44.7% — catastrophic loss. Most direct historical analogue to the current Hormuz crisis — same Persian Gulf origin, same long duration, and ultimately complete voter sentiment collapse.
  • 1990-91 Gulf War: oil $17→$41 (+141%), but only lasted 5 months. Bush 41 ultimately lost to Clinton — proving that brief oil shocks can have political aftereffects lasting 18+ months. Also Fair model's most famous failure case.
  • 2008 oil peak: oil $60→$147 (+145%), lasted 8 months. McCain lost by 7.4pp. Proves the destructive power of simultaneous economic data and voter perception deterioration.
  • 2022 Russia-Ukraine war: oil $90→$128, offset by Dobbs ruling. Proves cumulative inflation memory can persist into the next election cycle (Harris 2024 still bore the burden).

6. Dashboard capability boundaries

  • Does not output 2028 presidential state-level forecasts. Candidates undecided (Trump term-limited, open seat); primaries haven't begun; state-level data noise vastly exceeds signal 2.5 years out. Any specific 2028 state-level number is false precision.
  • Does not output 2026 House district-level forecasts. District-level forecast requires Cook PVI by district + candidate quality + incumbency variables, which require Cook Political Report subscription. The dashboard provides only state-level estimates.
  • Does not output single-point predictions like "X% probability R wins 2028". Three-model ensemble RMSE is ±2.5pp, plus additional uncertainty from being 2.5 years out — single-point estimates are misleading.
  • Does not predict non-economic issue effects. E.g., the Dobbs ruling (abortion) — non-economic variables are not in any model. That variable helped Democrats offset the oil shock in 2022 midterms; similar variables may reappear in 2026, but the models cannot capture them.
  • Does not predict black swan events. New 9/11-scale terror attacks, major natural disasters, major financial collapses — none are in the transmission chain.

7. Glossary

RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error)
The average magnitude by which a model's historical predictions deviate from true values. Fair model's RMSE of 2.5pp means: if it predicts the Republicans at 50%, the true value will be in the 45-55% range 95% of the time.
PVI (Cook Partisan Voting Index)
How a state or district leans relative to the national average. E.g., Maine's D+2 means Maine leans 2pp more Democratic than the national average.
ICS (Michigan Index of Consumer Sentiment)
The University of Michigan's monthly consumer sentiment survey, indexed to 1966=100. The current 53 is near 1980/2008 crisis-bottom levels.
Generic Ballot
"Regardless of candidate, will you vote Democrat or Republican in the next congressional election" — the polling-aggregated value. Core input to the Bafumi-Erikson-Wlezien model.
DPER
Fair model variable: sitting president personally running = +1 (D) / -1 (R), open seat = 0. R in 2028 = 0 (Trump term-limited), losing approximately 3-4pp incumbency advantage.
DUR
Fair model "duration penalty" variable: first term = 0, two or more consecutive terms ≥ 1. R in 2028 has only one term, so DUR = 0.
Z (Good News Quarters)
Fair model variable: number of quarters in the first 15 quarters of the administration where real per-capita GDP growth exceeded 3.2%.
Class II / Class III Senate
US senators serve 6-year terms divided into three rotating classes. 2026 elects Class II (22 R + 11 D); 2028 elects Class III (~21 R + 12 D).
Bafumi-Erikson-Wlezien (BEW)
The 2010 midterm House seat forecasting model by these three scholars, with Generic Ballot as core input variable.
